About Winawer Microchess

Szymon Winawer

Winawer Microchess, aka Knightless Microchess, is a simplified variant of chess stumbled upon by Jonathan Winawer and his 5-year-old daughter (5 and 3/4, she says) on April 4, 2026, during said daughter's bedtime.

Winawer learned chess as a kid but was never particularly good at it. In contrast, a distant, long-passed relative, Szymon Winawer, was one of the top players in the world in the 19th century. To honor that lineage, we named it Winawer Microchess.

The name Knightless Microchess is the alternate and more descriptive title. When we started tinkering, we found that wherever we put a knight, it made it possible for white to get a checkmate on the first move! To keep the game balanced and playable on a 4x4 grid, we removed the knights and doubled up the bishops instead.

The game was then vibe coded using Gemini 3.
